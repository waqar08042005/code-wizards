Prevention Tips

  • Keep personal details private: Avoid sharing too much personal information online, such as your home address, phone number, or financial details.
  • Use strong, unique passwords: Create passwords that are difficult to guess and use a different password for each account.
  • Enable two-factor authentication: Use two-factor authentication (2FA) for added security on accounts that support it, like email or social media.
  • Report suspicious behavior promptly: If someone is harassing or stalking you online, report it to the platform immediately. Document the behavior if possible.
  • Beware of phishing attempts: Do not click on links or open attachments in unsolicited emails. Phishing is a common method used by criminals to gain access to your personal information.
  • Monitor your digital footprint: Regularly search for your name online and review the information available about you. This helps you stay aware of any potentially harmful content.

Resources

If you're a victim of online stalking or blackmail, here are some trusted resources for support and guidance:

  • Cyberbullying Research Center: Learn about cyberbullying and its impact on victims.
  • Stop Stalking US: Resources and information to help those who are experiencing stalking.
  • Legal Help on Blackmail: Legal information about blackmail, including how to protect yourself and take action.
  • FBI Stalking Resources: Guidelines from the FBI on how to recognize and respond to stalking.
  • 1in6 - Sexual Assault Resources: A support center for male victims of sexual assault and related crimes, including stalking and blackmail.
